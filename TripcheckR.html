Crash data anlaysis TripCheck Twitter Feed

The data pulled is from 2013-04-08 to 2014-11-11. There are 900 raw data points.

Filtering on the crtieria: Santiam Pass Summit and crash Taking complete cases reduces the number of data points to 50.

The analysis also looks at meta incidents, in this case there were 86 days with snow. These data points are plotted along with the crash data.

The data cover a date range from 2013-04-01 to 2014-10-31 and there were 0.6055363 accidents per week.

Since the distance over which the accidents occured is 11 miles, the accident density is 2.8625354 accident/year/mile.

Timline of accidents

The graph below shows a timline of accidents, with the location of the accident (measured in distance from the summit) represented by the y-axis. The positive direction is West.

Blue data points represent days when snow was reported in the feed.

Histogram of distance from Santiam Pass Summit

This plot shows the distribution of accidents as measured by the distance from Santiam Pass Summit. In this specific case, while the number of accidents on the West side outweigh those on the East, there appears to be a strong bias to the distribution if one takes the mode as the center of the distribution.
